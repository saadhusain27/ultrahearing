Hearing Test

At our clinic, we provide comprehensive hearing tests to assess your hearing health and address any concerns you may have. Our hearing tests follow a three-step process to ensure accurate evaluation and personalized care.

History

The first step is gathering your history. We take the time to understand your unique experiences, symptoms, and concerns related to your hearing. This helps us gain valuable insights into any potential causes or factors that might be affecting your hearing abilities.

Testing

Next, we conduct a thorough hearing test. Using advanced diagnostic equipment, we measure your hearing sensitivity across different frequencies and determine the level of hearing loss, if any. This evaluation helps us identify the type, degree, and configuration of your hearing loss, enabling us to tailor our recommendations to your specific needs.

Consult

Once the hearing test is completed, we discuss the results with you. Our expert audiologists will explain the findings in a clear and understandable manner. We'll discuss the implications of the results and their impact on your daily life. Based on the outcome, we will explore the various treatment options available to you.

Treatment

The treatment options can vary depending on your individual needs and the nature of your hearing loss. They may include the fitting of hearing aids, which can improve your ability to hear and understand speech. For certain cases, assistive listening devices, such as Bluetooth accessories or telecoil systems, may be recommended. In some instances, medical intervention or referral to other specialists might be necessary. At our clinic, we are dedicated to providing comprehensive care and support throughout your hearing health journey. Our team will work closely with you to develop a personalized treatment plan that meets your specific needs and goals. We are committed to helping you rediscover the joy of hearing and improving your overall quality of life.

Wax Cleaning

Our ears, while remarkable, sometimes require assistance to maintain their peak performance. Ear wax, also known as cerumen, is produced by the cerumen gland which are present in the ear canal .It is normal and actually acts as a natural cleanser moving outward and gathering the dirt ,dead cells and hair along the way .Earwax buildup is a common issue that can lead to discomfort and hearing impairment. Don't hesitate to consult a hearing specialist if you experience symptoms of wax buildup.

Symptoms
  • Ear ache: Pain or discomfort in the affected ear.
  • Hearing loss: Reduced hearing or a feeling of muffled sound.
  • Tinnitus: Ringing, buzzing, or hissing sounds in the ear.
  • Ear fullness or blockage: A feeling of something stuck in the ear.
  • Itching: Itchy sensation in the ear canal.
  • Coughing: Sometimes, earwax can stimulate a cough reflex due to its proximity to the throat.
  • Odor or discharge: Foul-smelling discharge from the affected ear in severe cases.
Treatment

We offer various methods tailored to your earwax type and any existing ear conditions:

  • Irrigation: A gentle stream of water clears away troublesome earwax.
  • Instrumentation: Tiny, specialized instruments, like a fine-tuned curette, delicately extract earwax, restoring your auditory canal's clarity.

Let us restore your hearing using the method that best suits your unique needs.

Hearing Aid Fitting

Hearing aid fitting is a process which helps determine if the hearing aid meets your needs and your style. Based on the result of your hearing test our hearing professional will recommend hearing aids and help you in selecting the latest technology aids which suits your hearing needs and lifestyle.

During the fitting appointment our hearing specialist will ensure that the hearing aid are programmed with accuracy to meet your need precisely. They should be comfortable, the sound is appropriate to your hearing loss and needs. You will learn how to insert and remove them, how to clean and take care of them.

You will have a follow up appointment after two weeks of fitting where you can share your experience about the hearing aid in different situations. If you have any concerns or are not able to hear properly in any situation we will reprogram your hearing aids .

Custom Earplugs

Everyone knows that prevention is better than cure.One of the leading cause of preventable hearing loss is exposure to loud noise known as noise induced hearing loss (NIHL).NIHL is caused by continuous exposure to loud sounds over an extended period of time .This is the type of hearing loss which can be prevented by using ear plugs or other protective devices.

Custom Hearing protection

Those who are exposed to noise level at or over 85 dB for at least 8 hr for a long period of time. Suitable for construction crews, industrial workers, hunters, during concerts and sporting events.

Musician plugs

Whether you are a musician or love to listen to music, these custom made earplugs help your ear from exposure to loud sound while preserving the clarity and quality of music.

Swimming earplugs

If you are a professional swimmer or just like to swim for fun ,swimmer earplugs protect your ear by creating a tight seal and preventing water from getting into your ear canals and causing an infection.recommed for people who get repeated ear infection or has tubes in their ears.
