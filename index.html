    Hearing Test

    We provide hearing tests FREE-OF-CHARGE to anyone 18 years of age and older. These tests are available without a referral.

    All hearing tests at Omni Hearing are conducted by a licensed hearing care professional.

    Each test takes only thirty minutes and can provide us with a wealth of information about the current state of your hearing health.

    We also welcome family members and friends to attend hearing tests so they can ask questions and provide us with any information the individual might forget to mention. The more information we have, the better we can determine your hearing care needs.

    When you come for a hearing care appointment at Omni Hearing, we will begin by having you fill out a case history questionnaire. This will be followed by an ear canal examination (otoscopy) and series of comprehensive hearing tests, including: impedance audiometry, pure tone audiometry and speech audiometry.?Omni Hearing will compile the results of your hearing evaluation.

    Wax Cleaning

    Ear wax, also known as cerumen, is entirely ordinary and necessary. The glands naturally produce it to moisten the ear canals to protect the eardrum from dust and debris, even insects. Earwax typically clears itself from the ears, but it can buildup and cause blockages in some cases.​

    First, our fully certified hearing specialists will have a look in your ear canals with an otoscope to examine the extent of earwax buildup – our locations have a video otoscope that allows you to see inside your ear canal on the screen. If the earwax buildup is significant, we will begin the wax removal process using best-practice techniques.

    Tinnitus Management

    Tinnitus and hearing loss often go hand in hand, with approximately 80% of people suffering from tinnitus also having hearing loss. It is one of the most common health conditions globally, affecting between 10% and 15% of the global population regularly. Hearing loss may affect the signals sent from the ear to the brain. Those simulated signals can result in unwanted activity in the brain that is known as tinnitus. While tinnitus doesn’t cause hearing loss, the perceived sound can distract and make it hard to concentrate on other sounds. That’s why some of the best hearing aids for tinnitus can be an effective way for tinnitus management as well as hearing loss.

    Types of Hearing Aids

    Hearing aids are an amazing, non-invasive solution to helping with hearing loss. Many people say that since they started wearing them, their quality of life has dramatically changed, and the majority of them say they wish they even had started wearing them earlier. The benefits are felt in every area of a person’s life. For example, these devices will boost your confidence, make you enjoy going out to noisy restaurants again, and your brain will thank you. They will help you hear what you want too, while automatically reducing background noise.


    There are 5 main types of hearing aids, all of which are available at Ultra Hearing Clinic.

    In The Ear

    ITE hearing aids are custom made and fill the outer ear. They are recommended for those with mild to severe hearing loss

    In The Canal

    In the Canal hearing aids are custom made and rest in the ear canal. They are recommended for those with mild to moderately severe hearing loss

    Completly in the Canal

    CIC hearing aids are custom made and rest deep inside the ear canal. They are the smallest of the custom made hearing aids, therefore making them very discrete. They are recommended for those with mild to moderate hearing loss

    Behind the Ear

    BTE hearing aids rest behind the ear and transmit sound via a thin tube to the ear. They are recommended for those with mild to profound hearing loss

    Open Behind the Ear

    OBTE hearing aids rest behind the ear and are slightly more discreet than other behind the ear models. They are recommended for those with mild to severe hearing loss

    Frequently Asked Questioins

    • Why is it important to correct hearing loss?

      A good sense of hearing is critical to our safety and a high quality of our life. Our sense of hearing helps to connect us to the world around us and is critical for effective communication in every aspect of our life. Hearing loss can not only lead to feelings of isolation and frustration, but also hinder us from enjoying the simple pleasures of life such as hearing the laughter of a loved one. The sooner you get your hearing checked and are on your way to finding a solution, the sooner you can increase your, and your loved ones’ quality of life.

    • How do I know if I am experiencing hearing loss?

      There are many clues to look for when it comes to experiencing hearing loss. Common symptoms include but are not limited to thinking that people around you are mumbling, difficulty hearing in crowds, turning up the television, and asking people to repeat themselves.

    • Which hearing aid is right for me?

      There are many factors to be considered when choosing the hearing aid that suits you best. This includes the level of hearing loss, lifestyle, dexterity and your personal preference. Our specialist will help you decide the best course of action based on these factors when it comes to choosing the right hearing aid(s) for you.

    • Do I need hearing aids for both ear's?

      This depends on whether you have hearing loss in one or both ears. If you happen to have hearing loss in both ears, then it would be of great benefit to you to get two hearing aids. This would help you with understanding speech better and better perceiving the directions that sounds come from.
